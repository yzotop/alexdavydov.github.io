Irreversibility

Committing closes doors.

Option value collapses when choices become hard to undo.

Irreversibility is not about drama. It is about the cost of changing your mind. When a decision is hard to reverse, waiting and learning become valuable — because mistakes become expensive.

A system can look optimal in the short run while it burns optionality. The long-run loss is not visible in the metric — it is visible in the paths you can no longer take.

Irreversible choices convert uncertainty into fate.

The value of information is highest before commitment. After commitment, optimization often becomes defense: protecting a path you cannot easily leave.

Good decisions do not only pick an action. They preserve the right to change it.
