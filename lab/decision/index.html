Decision

Choices under uncertainty.

Decisions in adaptive systems trade immediate reward for information. The best choice now may not be the best choice later, once uncertainty resolves.

Exploration collects information at a cost. Exploitation harvests reward but may miss better options. The tension between learning and earning shapes long-run performance.

Exploration vs Exploitation
Learning costs. Not learning costs more.
Choices trade reward now for information later.
Regret
Knowing later. Choosing earlier.
The cost of alternatives revealed too late.
Value of Information
Pay now. Decide better later.
Learning has diminishing returns. The optimum is where value beats cost.
Irreversibility
Committing closes doors.
When choices are hard to undo, information is most valuable before you act.

Good decisions optimize the learning curve, not just the next step. In uncertain systems, information is a form of revenue.
