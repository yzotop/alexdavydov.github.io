Regret

Knowing later. Choosing earlier.

The cost of alternatives revealed too late.

Regret measures the gap between what was chosen and what could have been chosen, once uncertainty resolves. It is not a mistake at decision time — it is a comparison made after the fact.

In uncertain systems, regret accumulates quietly. Early choices look reasonable. The cost appears only in hindsight, when the unrealized path becomes visible.

Regret is invisible while decisions are made. It becomes measurable only after alternatives are known.

Minimizing immediate loss can maximize long-run regret. Good decisions do not eliminate regret — they shape how fast it grows.
