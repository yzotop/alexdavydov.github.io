Pressure

What pushes systems to change.

Pressure is not an event. It is a force that reshapes transitions before outcomes change.

In adaptive systems, pressure accumulates quietly. It redirects flows, concentrates mass, and creates asymmetry long before metrics react.

Pressure changes how systems move, not just where they end up.

Reshaping
When constraints bend distributions.
Pressure redistributes mass without creating new states.
Threshold
Small change. Big effect.
Crossing a threshold can flip dominant behavior.
Saturation
More pressure, less response.
Beyond a point, added force no longer moves the system.
Intervention
Acting on pressure, not outcomes.
Interventions reshape constraints before they reshape metrics.

Time explains when change becomes visible. Pressure explains why it happens at all.

Interventions act on pressure, not on outcomes.
