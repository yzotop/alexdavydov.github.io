Markov Lens

A way to see adaptive systems.

The Markov lens is not a model, but a perspective: systems are described by states and transitions, without memory, yet complex long-run behavior emerges.

Core theses
Memoryless does not mean simple.
Local rules can produce global structure.
Transitions matter more than labels.
Pressure reshapes flows before it reshapes outcomes.
Equilibrium is a fate, not a goal.
Stability and breakdown are often separated by long silence.

The exhibits show states, transitions, distributions, and equilibrium as different projections of the same system.
