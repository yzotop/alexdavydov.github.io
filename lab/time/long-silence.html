Long Silence

Nothing happens. Until it does.

Stable metrics can coexist with rising risk of a sudden shift.

A system can look quiet while internal pressure builds. What we observe can remain stable even as the probability of a shift increases. Silence is not evidence of stability. It can be a state.

By the time the shift is visible in the observed metric, it may be too late. Timing uncertainty lives in what we do not observe.
