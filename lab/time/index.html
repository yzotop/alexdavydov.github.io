Time

When change happens.

Time is not the x-axis. It is uncertainty about when structure becomes visible. A system can follow stable rules and still hide its turning points for a long time.

In adaptive systems, change is often real long before it is measurable. Silence is not evidence of stability — it can be a state of the system.

Waiting Time
When something must happen, but not now.
Even with fixed rules, the time to a transition can be wildly variable.
Long Silence
Nothing happens. Until it does.
Stable metrics can coexist with rising risk of a sudden shift.
Drift
Change without events.
Small biases in transitions slowly reshape distributions without a clear moment.
Regime Change
Before and after look reasonable.
Regimes become obvious in hindsight; the boundary rarely is.

Time completes the Markov lens: transitions explain movement, distributions show shape, equilibrium describes fate — and time explains when these become observable.
