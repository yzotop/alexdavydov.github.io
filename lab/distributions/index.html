Distributions

The geometry of mass.

Distributions describe how mass is arranged across states, without history.

Shapes matter: tails, peaks, regimes, concentration.

Uniform (1–100)
A baseline shape.
Equal mass across states.
Divisors (1–100)
Structure inside integers.
A state feature reshapes the distribution.
Heavy Tail
Most mass lives in the tail.
Zipf-like concentration across states.
Bimodal
Two regimes. One space.
A mixture of peaks creates regimes.
