Curvature of Decisions

Small choices. Large bends.

Tiny actions can strongly deform long-run trajectories.

Decisions do not move systems in straight lines. Each choice slightly bends the future trajectory. Early curvature compounds over time.

Two paths can start close and diverge far without shocks. Curvature explains why local optimization changes global behavior.

Direction matters more than speed.

Systems rarely fail by sudden force. They drift into trouble by accumulated curvature. Local decisions feel reversible. Global paths are not.

Most irreversibility is geometric.
