Feasible Set

Constraints define the world.

What you can't do matters as much as what you can.

The feasible set is the space of actions and states the system can actually reach.

Optimization assumes freedom. Reality supplies constraints.

Constraints define the world before decisions define the path. Two strategies can look equally good on paper, but live in different feasible sets.

When constraints tighten, the 'best' option may disappear. The system does not move to a worse choice — it moves to the best remaining choice.

Optionality is geometry. Metrics are shadows.

Good work is often not improving the number. It is enlarging — or correctly mapping — what is feasible.
