Uncertainty Surface

Where the system is sensitive.

Some regions amplify small changes. Others absorb them.

Uncertainty is not uniform. Some regions of the state space are flat: actions barely matter. Other regions are steep: tiny changes flip outcomes.

The surface describes sensitivity, not probability. Outcomes change when trajectories cross high-curvature regions. Risk concentrates where the surface is steep, not where variance looks large.

Uncertainty has topology, not just magnitude.

Systems often look stable until they move onto a steep part of the surface. Metrics lag geometry. Stability is local, not global.

Regime change is geometric before it is statistical.
