Option Space Collapse

Commitment reshapes the future.

A decision can collapse reachable futures before any metric moves.

Before a decision, many futures remain reachable. After commitment, some paths become impossible. The collapse happens immediately — even if the observed metric stays unchanged.

This is why waiting can be valuable. Information gathered before commitment preserves optionality. Information gathered after commitment only optimizes within a smaller space.

Option space collapses at the moment of commitment. Metrics respond later.

The cost of premature commitment is invisible in metrics. It appears only in the futures you can no longer reach. Good decisions preserve optionality until information becomes actionable.
