Reachability vs Optimality

The best solution you cannot reach is irrelevant.

Optimization assumes access. Reality imposes reachability.

Optimality is defined on paper. Reachability is defined by history, constraints, and prior decisions. Systems do not choose the best point. They choose the best reachable point.

Geometry decides what "best" means before metrics do.

An unreachable optimum does not exist.

Many failures are framed as poor optimization. Most are failures of reachability. Metrics optimize locally. Geometry limits globally.

Optimization happens inside geometry.
