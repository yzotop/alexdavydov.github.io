Transitions

How systems move.

Transitions describe how systems move from one state to another. Even without memory, movement can be directional, asymmetric, and structured.

By looking at transitions directly, we see not outcomes, but mechanisms. Flows reveal pressure, friction, and hidden constraints inside the system.
